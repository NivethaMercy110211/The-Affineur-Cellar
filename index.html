Meet the Affineur

A Life Devoted
to the Cellar

I am Emile Vaucher — affineur, cellar keeper, and lifelong student of the wheel. My craft began in the limestone caves of Franche-Comte, where I learned that great cheese is not made but coaxed — with brushes, brine, patience, and time.

At The Affineur Cellar, every wheel that enters our cave is treated as a living thing. We monitor humidity, turn each wheel by hand, and listen to the slow conversation between milk, salt, and stone.

The Cave Process

From Milk to Masterpiece —
The Art of Affinage

Every great aged cheese is shaped by its environment as much as its recipe. Our cave is not just a room — it is an instrument.

Reception & Salting

Fresh wheels arrive at our cave within 48 hours of pressing. We assess, dry-salt or brine-bathe each one to guide rind formation and control moisture loss.

Turning & Brushing

Wheels are turned every 2–4 days and hand-brushed with brine washes, wine, beer, or custom herb solutions to develop the rind's unique character.

Aging & Maturation

The cave works its quiet magic — temperature gradients across the cellar produce different results for each style, from 45-day chevres to 24-month alpines.
